Fourier Visualization Tool

This tool visualizes how functions can be approximated using Fourier series.

Given a real-valued function \$f(x)\$ defined on the interval \$[0, L]\$, this tool visualizes the construction of its Fourier sine series, given by:

\$$ \sum_{n=1}^\infty \,b_n \sin{\frac{n \pi x}{L}}\$$

where:

\$$ b_n = \frac{2}{L} \int_0^L \!f(x) \sin{\frac{n \pi x}{L}} \:dx\$$

The graph space below shows the graphs of the given function \$f(x)\$ along with the approximating Fourier series partial sum. For each term of the series, this tool sketches the graph of just that term, as a single sine wave. As each new term is added, the partial Fourier series gets more complete. The first term of the Fourier series is a partial sum on its own. Then, each subsequent term of the series is drawn one-by-one as its own simple sine wave. After the graph of a term's wave is drawn, the heights of that new wave are shown as vertical lines extending from the wave to the \$x\$-axis. These heights then move to anchor themselves to the graph of the existing partial Fourier series, and a new wave is drawn from these heights, depicting the sum of the previous partial sum and the new term as a new wave representing a more complete partial sum, and more closely matching the graph of \$f(x)\$ on the interval from \$0\$ to \$L\$.
